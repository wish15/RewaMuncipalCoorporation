About Rewa

Rewa, known as the "Land of White Tigers," is a historic city in the heart of Madhya Pradesh, India. The city serves as the administrative headquarters of Rewa district and is famous for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and significant contribution to India's wildlife conservation efforts. Rewa was the former capital of the princely state of Rewa and has played a crucial role in the region's political and cultural development.

Located in the northeastern part of Madhya Pradesh, Rewa is strategically positioned and well-connected to major cities in central India. The city is renowned worldwide for being the place where the first white tiger was discovered in 1951, which led to a successful breeding program that helped preserve this rare genetic variant. The Govindgarh Palace and the beautiful Keoti Falls are among the many attractions that draw visitors to this enchanting city.

Rewa, the Historic Princely State

Rewa has a glorious history dating back to the 13th century when it was established as a princely state. The Baghela Rajputs founded the kingdom of Rewa, and it remained an important princely state throughout the medieval and British colonial periods. The rulers of Rewa were known for their patronage of arts, culture, and learning, making the city a center of intellectual and cultural activities.

The last ruler of Rewa, Maharaja Martand Singh, played a significant role in India's independence movement and was instrumental in the integration of Rewa into the Indian Union in 1948. The Govindgarh Palace, built by the royal family, stands as a testament to the architectural grandeur and rich heritage of the region. The palace complex showcases a beautiful blend of Mughal and European architectural styles.

Rewa, the Land of Natural Beauty

Rewa district is located in the northeastern region of Madhya Pradesh, bordered by Uttar Pradesh to the north and northeast. The district lies between 24°18' to 25°12' north latitude and 81°02' to 82°18' east longitude. The region is characterized by undulating terrain with the Vindhya Range running through the southern part of the district.

The Son River, one of the major tributaries of the Ganges, flows through the district, providing irrigation and supporting agriculture. The climate is tropical with three distinct seasons: summer, monsoon, and winter. The average annual rainfall is about 1,200 mm, making it suitable for various crops and supporting rich biodiversity.

Demographics of Rewa

According to the 2011 Census, Rewa district has a population of 2,365,106 people, making it one of the more populous districts in Madhya Pradesh. The city of Rewa serves as the urban center with a population of approximately 236,000. The district has a literacy rate of 73.42%, which is above the national average, reflecting the region's emphasis on education and development.

The population density is 375 persons per square kilometer. The sex ratio is 931 females per 1000 males. The majority of the population is engaged in agriculture and allied activities, while a significant portion is employed in government services, education, and small-scale industries. The district has a rich cultural diversity with people from various communities living in harmony.

Rewa, the Cultural Heritage Hub

Rewa is a treasure trove of cultural heritage, with its roots deeply embedded in Indian traditions and customs. The city has been a patron of classical music, dance, and literature for centuries. The Rewa Gharana of Hindustani classical music is world-renowned, having produced legendary musicians like Ustad Allauddin Khan and Pandit Ravi Shankar.

The region celebrates numerous festivals with great enthusiasm, including Diwali, Holi, Dussehra, and local festivals like Karma and Teej. The traditional folk dances like Karma, Saila, and Rajkumari are integral parts of the cultural celebrations. The local cuisine features traditional dishes like Dal Bafla, Poha, and various sweets that reflect the rich culinary heritage of the region.

Economic Profile of Rewa

Rewa's economy is primarily based on agriculture, with the fertile plains of the Son River supporting extensive cultivation. The major crops include wheat, rice, sugarcane, gram, and various pulses. The district is also known for its mineral resources, including limestone, coal, and bauxite, which support local industries.

The city has been developing as an educational hub with several institutions of higher learning, including the Maharaja of Rewa Autonomous State University (now APS University). The service sector, including government offices, educational institutions, and healthcare facilities, provides significant employment opportunities. Small-scale industries and handicrafts also contribute to the local economy.

Administrative Structure

Rewa district is administered by a Collector who is the chief administrative officer. The district is divided into several tehsils and blocks for effective governance. The Rewa Municipal Corporation oversees the urban areas, while the Zilla Panchayat manages rural development programs.

The administrative setup includes various departments working in coordination to provide essential services to citizens. The district administration focuses on development programs, law and order, revenue collection, and implementation of government schemes for the welfare of the people.

Tourist Attractions

Rewa offers a unique blend of historical monuments, natural beauty, and wildlife attractions. The city is internationally famous for the White Tiger Safari at Mukundpur, where visitors can see the descendants of the world's first captive white tiger, Mohan.
