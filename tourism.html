Tourism

There are many tourist places in Rewa and surrounding areas that make Rewa a prime destination for tourism. Rewa is known as the "Land of White Tigers" and offers a unique blend of natural beauty, historical significance, and cultural heritage. If you would like to visit nearby tourist places near Rewa, destinations like Bandhavgarh, Khajuraho, Chitrakoot, Amarkantak, etc. are worth a visit.

The city is a blend of old and modern architecture. There are many places for tourists in Rewa like White Tiger Safari, Govindgarh Palace, Keoti Falls, Chachai Falls, Bahuti Falls, Rani Talab, Venkat Bhawan, Rewa Fort, and many historical and natural attractions that captivate visitors from around the world.

Rewa is located in the northeastern part of Madhya Pradesh, surrounded by natural beauty and rich biodiversity. The district is famous for its natural parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and historical monuments. The main attractions include White Tiger Safari at Mukundpur, beautiful waterfalls, ancient temples, and the magnificent Govindgarh Palace.

Places of Interest

Must Visit

White Tiger Safari

Mukundpur, Rewa

World's first White Tiger Safari featuring descendants of Mohan, the first captive white tiger. Home to rare white tigers and other wildlife species.

9:00 AM - 5:00 PM Entry Fee: ₹50

Govindgarh Palace

Govindgarh, Rewa

Magnificent palace built by the royal family of Rewa. Showcases beautiful architecture and houses a museum with royal artifacts.

10:00 AM - 6:00 PM Entry Fee: ₹30

Keoti Falls

25 km from Rewa

Beautiful waterfall cascading from a height of 98 feet. Popular picnic spot surrounded by lush greenery and natural beauty.

Best time: Monsoon Trekking available

Chachai Falls

46 km from Rewa

One of the highest waterfalls in Madhya Pradesh, falling from 130 feet height. Spectacular view during monsoon season.

More Tourist Places

Bahuti Falls

About 65 km from Rewa, Bahuti Falls is a magnificent waterfall surrounded by dense forests. Popular for its pristine natural beauty and peaceful environment.

Rani Talab

Historic pond built during the royal era. Beautiful architecture with steps leading to the water. A peaceful place for evening walks and photography.

Chitrakoot

Sacred pilgrimage site located 130 km from Rewa. Associated with Lord Rama's exile period. Beautiful ghats along the Mandakini River.

Bandhavgarh National Park

Famous tiger reserve located 140 km from Rewa. Home to the highest density of tigers in India. Excellent for wildlife photography and jungle safaris.

Culinary Delights

Rewa offers a rich culinary experience with traditional Bundelkhandi and Bagheli cuisine. The local food reflects the cultural heritage and agricultural abundance of the region.

Traditional Dishes

Dal Bafla

Traditional wheat dumplings served with spicy dal and ghee. A signature dish of the region.

Poha

Flattened rice cooked with onions, spices, and garnished with sev and lemon.

Kachori

Deep-fried bread stuffed with spiced lentils, served with tamarind chutney.

Sweets & Desserts

Kheer

Traditional rice pudding made with milk, sugar, and dry fruits.

Jalebi

Crispy spiral-shaped sweet soaked in sugar syrup, a local favorite.

Malpua

Sweet pancake made with flour, milk, and cardamom, served during festivals.
